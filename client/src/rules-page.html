Rules Drunkopoly

  • Rules player
  • Each player starts with $1500
  • Rules banker
  • The banker is the auctioneer
  • The banker keeps his funds separate from the bank
  • Rules bank
  • The bank holds title deed cards, barrels and breweries
  • The bank pays salaries and bonuses
  • The bank sells and auctions properties and gives the correct title deed cards
  • The bank sells barrels and breweries
  • The bank loans money on mortgages
  • The bank collects taxes, fines, loans and interest
  • Rules play
  • Place your token on the go corner
  • Next throwing player is the player on the left of the previous thrower
  • Multiple players can rest on the same space at the same time
  • You may be entitled to buy the real estate or other properties
  • Pay the bill when the property is already sold
  • Pay taxes
  • Take a chance or community card
  • If you roll double, the throwing player can throw again. If the player throws doubles 3 times after each other, the player goes to jail
  • When passing the “go” field, the player receives $200
  • Rules for buying
  • If the player lands on a property, he can buy it from the bank for the printed price
  • If you don’t want to buy the property, it gets sold to the highest bidder
  • Anyone can participate in the auction, even the player who didn’t want to buy the property in the first place
  • Bidding prices may start from any price
  • Rules for paying the bill
  • When landing on an owned property, the player pays the other player the bill for drinking at his/her property
  • If the property is mortgaged, there is no bill to be paid
  • If a player owns all colours of a specific street, the bill is doubled if there are no barrel build on any of the properties
  • If a player owns all colours of a specific street, he may build barrels and breweries to increase the bills
  • The owner may not be able to collect the money if the next player has thrown before asking for the money
  • Rules for chance and community chest
  • When landing on each of the spaces, the player takes the top card of the space he landed on
  • Follow the instructions on the card
  • Only the “Get out of jail free” card can be kept by the player
  • Rules for income taxes
  • There are 2 options:
  • The player pays the bank $200
  • The player pays the bank 10% of what the player is worth (this includes cash in hand, printed prices of mortgaged and unmortgaged properties and cost prices of all owned buildings)
  • The player can choose what option he takes
  • Rules jail
  • You go to jail when:
  • You land on the “go to jail” space
  • You draw the card “go to jail”
  • You throw doubles 3 times in a row
  • You can’t collect your salary and bill when sent to jail
  • How to bail:
  • Throw doubles in any of the next 3 turns, if succeeded you can move those spaces
  • Use the “get out of jail free” card
  • Purchase the “get out of jail free” card from another player
  • Pay the bank $50 and roll the dice
  • Rules free parking
  • This is a rest spot, no money is awarded
  • Rules barrels
  • Only when a full street is owned, the player can start building barrels
  • The barrels has to be spread evenly, meaning there can’t be 2 Barrels on 1 property and 0 on another
  • If there are no Barrels left, the player has to wait for another player to sell their barrels
  • The player can still collect double when there is no barrel on 1 property but there are barrels on the other properties
  • Rules breweries
  • When a player has 4 barrels, he can exchange it for a brewery
  • Only 1 brewery can be built on 1 property
  • Rules building shortage
  • When there are no more barrels, the player has to wait on another player to sell theirs
  • If 2 players want to buy at once and there are not enough barrels, they may be auctioned
  • Rules selling property
  • Spaces where you can’t build on, can be sold privately at anytime
  • Before a player can sell a card to another player, he has to sell all barrels and breweries
  • Barrels and breweries will be sold at any time for half the price to the bank
  • All barrels must be sold evenly
  • Rules Mortgage
  • Unimproved properties can be mortgaged through the bank
  • Before an improved property can be sold, the player has to sell all the barrels and breweries first
  • If the player wants to unmortgage a property, he has to pay 10% interest on top of the original price
  • The mortgage property can be sold to another player
  • Same rules apply for the buyer of the mortgage card, like if he was the player who mortgaged the property
  • Rules Bankruptcy
  • You are declared bankrupt if you can’t pay another player or the bank with everything you got (cash, properties, barrels, breweries)
  • Rules miscellaneous
  • A player can only take a loan from the bank
  • Rules for short game
  • Each player gets 3 random title deed cards
  • You only need 3 barrels instead of 4 to build a brewery
  • If in jail you must exit the next turn with 1 of the 3 options
  • Income tax is always $200
  • End of game: game ends when one player is bankrupt
